# Course Overview

**Course:** IST 110 – Information, People & Technology  
**Institution:** Penn State World Campus  
**Semester:** Spring 2026

---

IST 110 provided a broad introduction to how information technology intersects with people and organizations. Throughout the semester, major topics included:

- **Cybersecurity fundamentals** — threats, social engineering, phishing, and best practices
- **Privacy and ethics** — the privacy paradox, database systems, and the ethical use of data
- **Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)** — how publicly available information can be gathered and analyzed
- **Digital literacy and communication** — structured discussion, research, and academic writing
- **Crowdsourcing** — collective intelligence, real-world applications, and organizational benefits
- **IT in Industry** — how technology has transformed operations across healthcare, retail, and manufacturing
- **Portfolio and professional development** — building a digital presence using platforms like GitHub

This course built a strong conceptual foundation for my studies in cybersecurity and gave me real-world context for why information security matters.
