# L07: CODIS Cold Case

**Module:** L07  
**Points:** 15  
**Type:** Individual Assignment

---

## Description

In this assignment I researched the Combined DNA Index System (CODIS), the FBI's national DNA database used to match profiles across forensic, arrestee, detainee, offender, and missing person indices. I visited the FBI website, searched for a CODIS cold case that was solved using DNA evidence, and wrote a summary addressing how DNA sampling contributed to solving the case, when and how the DNA was collected, and what should be done with samples from people who are never convicted.

## Reflection

This assignment sat right at the intersection of databases, privacy, and real-world law enforcement, which made it genuinely compelling. The power of CODIS to link cold cases decades later is remarkable, but the ethical questions around retaining DNA from people who are arrested but never convicted are serious and not easy to dismiss. It connected well to the privacy themes running through the rest of the course and made me think more carefully about who controls data and what the long-term consequences of that control can be.
