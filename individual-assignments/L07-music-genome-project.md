# L07: Music Genome Project

**Module:** L07  
**Points:** 10  
**Type:** Individual Assignment

---

## Description

For this assignment I explored Pandora's Music Genome Project, a database that classifies music by hundreds of attributes to power its recommendation engine. I visited the Pandora website, entered a seed artist, listened to five songs the station played, and answered questions about how the data is collected and used, what musical categories were applied, and what those categories reveal about the underlying database structure.

## Reflection

This was one of the more enjoyable assignments of the semester because it connected something I use in my daily life to the database concepts we were studying. I had never thought about Pandora as a database application before, but that is exactly what it is. The Music Genome Project is essentially a massive, carefully structured dataset where every song is tagged across hundreds of attributes and the recommendation engine queries that data in real time. It made abstract database concepts like categorization, indexing, and querying feel very concrete and relevant.
