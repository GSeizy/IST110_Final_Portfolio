# Reflections

---

## Overall Experience

IST 110 covered a lot more ground than I expected going in. I came into this course thinking it would be mostly foundational stuff I already had a passing knowledge of, but topics like OSINT, the privacy paradox, and the deeper mechanics of how the internet actually works pushed me to think more critically than I anticipated. The discussion posts in particular forced me to do real research and form actual arguments rather than just stating opinions, which is a skill I know I will carry forward.

## Challenges Faced

The biggest challenge this semester was honestly time. Working full time on second shift while taking two courses simultaneously does not leave a lot of margin for error. There were weeks where balancing work, school, and everything else felt nearly impossible. The portfolio itself is a good example of that. I had the outline done early in the semester and fully intended to update it as I went, but life got in the way and it became one of those things that was out of sight, out of mind until the deadline hit. That is something I want to be more disciplined about going forward. On the content side, the data analytics module gave me the most trouble conceptually, specifically wrapping my head around the difference between predictive and prescriptive analytics until I found the right analogy to make it click.

## Connection to My Future Career

Almost everything covered in this course connects directly to where I want to go in cybersecurity. Phishing and social engineering are among the most common attack vectors in the real world, and being able to explain them clearly to a non-technical audience is a skill that matters in this field. OSINT is actively used by security professionals for threat intelligence and investigation. The privacy paradox gave me a much sharper understanding of why people make the security decisions they do, which is essential context for anyone working to protect systems and the people who use them. IST 110 gave me the big picture view of the field that my more technical courses build on top of, and I think that foundation is going to matter a lot as I move forward.
